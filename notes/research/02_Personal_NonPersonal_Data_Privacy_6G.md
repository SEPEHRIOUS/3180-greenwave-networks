# Paper Summary

**Full Citation:** Personal vs. Non-Personal Data Privacy in 6G Networks: Mechanisms, Compliance, and Architectural Patterns (2026). Systematic review of 78 peer-reviewed studies (2019-2025). https://doi.org/10.3390/app16104604

### Summary
This is a systematic review synthesizing 78 peer-reviewed studies published 
between 2019 and 2025 on privacy preservation in 6G networks. It distinguishes 
between personal data (individually identifiable) and non-personal data 
(aggregated/anonymized), and reviews privacy-enhancing technologies, regulatory 
compliance frameworks (like GDPR), and architectural design patterns used to 
protect each type across the reviewed literature.

## Relevance to Our Survey
This directly supports our regulatory/governance angle — it connects the 
technical privacy tools (e.g. differential privacy, federated learning) to the 
legal frameworks that are supposed to govern them, which ties into the 
"regulatory fragmentation" point I want to make when comparing this to the 
Consumer Trust literature review.

## Critical Comment
As a large-scale systematic review, it likely summarizes trends rather than 
deeply evaluating any single technical approach — worth checking whether it 
makes concrete recommendations or mainly maps the existing landscape.
