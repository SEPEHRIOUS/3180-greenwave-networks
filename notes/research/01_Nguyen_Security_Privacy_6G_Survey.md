# Paper Summary

**Full Citation:** Nguyen, V.-L., Lin, P.-C., Cheng, B.-C., Hwang, R.-H., & Lin, Y.-D. (2021). Security and Privacy for 6G: A Survey on Prospective Technologies and Challenges. IEEE Communications Surveys & Tutorials, 23(4), 2384-2428. https://arxiv.org/abs/2108.11861

## Summary
This survey maps out security and privacy challenges across three layers of 6G 
networks: the physical layer, the connection layer, and the service layer. The 
authors argue that 6G doesn't just inherit vulnerabilities from 5G — new radio 
technologies introduce fresh threat vectors, such as ultra-massive MIMO systems 
at Terahertz frequencies exposing radio equipment locations, and new attack 
surfaces created by pervasive AI embedded in the network. The paper proposes a 
set of mitigation techniques (physical layer protection, quantum-safe 
communication, AI-driven security, differential privacy) as the most promising 
directions for future defenses.

## Relevance to Our Survey
This paper is foundational for our Privacy Risks section because it gives a 
structured way to think about *where* privacy risks originate in 6G, rather than 
treating "privacy" as one flat problem. It's useful for framing the layered 
nature of the risks before narrowing into the AI-specific and consumer-facing 
angles our survey focuses on.

## Critical Comment
The survey is broad and technical/infrastructure-focused rather than 
consumer-facing — it says relatively little about how these risks are 
experienced or perceived by end users, which is the gap our own survey tries to 
help fill.
